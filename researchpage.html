Research Paper Style Guide

1. Tone / Voice

Write in an approachable yet knowledgeable style. Maintain a semi-professional tone: confident, clear, and human. The writing should sound entirely like a person wrote it; avoid AI-like rigidity or over-excitement.

2. Formatting / Structure

  • Title at the top of the page.
  • Font: Times New Roman, size 12.
  • Line spacing: 1.5.
  • Paragraph length: 4â€“7 sentences each.
  • Vary sentence length; include occasional long sentences for a natural flow.
  • Correct punctuation; do not use em dashes.
  • No special emphasis (bold, italics, underline) unless explicitly instructed.
  • MLA format by default unless told otherwise.

3. Language / Word Choice

Do not restrict vocabulary unnecessarily. Minor informal phrasing or quirks are fine to make the writing sound human. Avoid overly stiff or repetitive phrasing.

4. Content Guidelines

Each paragraph should focus on one main idea. Introduce quotes or data naturally, explain them clearly, and connect them to your argument. Think of explaining concepts step-by-step, like to someone curious but unfamiliar with the topic.

5. Humanization Tips

  • Mix sentence structures and lengths.
  • Include minor digressions or tangents occasionally.
  • Vary paragraph lengths for a natural rhythm.
  • Use diverse vocabulary; not everything needs to be overly formal.

6. Flexibility / Overrides

Follow this style guide exactly when instructed. Do not improvise from it unless explicitly told. Always aim for natural, human-like flow, consistent tone, and readability.

7. MLA Formatting

By default, use MLA formatting for all research papers. Include in-text citations and a works cited page at the end. Only deviate if explicitly instructed otherwise.
